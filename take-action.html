Take Action

Small changes in your shopping habits can make a big difference. Here's how you can help reduce fashion's environmental impact.

The 5 R's of Sustainable Fashion

A framework for making better choices

1

Reduce

Buy less. Before purchasing, ask yourself: "Do I really need this? Will I wear it at least 30 times?" Resist impulse buys and trend-driven shopping.

2

Rewear

Maximize what you already own. Get creative with styling, organize your closet to rediscover forgotten pieces, and challenge yourself to outfit repeating.

3

Repair

Fix before you throw. Learn basic mending skills, find a local tailor, or attend repair cafes. A small fix can add years to a garment's life.

4

Resell/Donate

Give clothes a second life. Sell on secondhand platforms, donate to local charities, or organize clothing swaps with friends.

5

Recycle

As a last resort, recycle textiles properly. Many brands offer take-back programs, and some municipalities have textile recycling bins.

Actionable ways to reduce your fashion footprint

Be intentional about every purchase.

  • Calculate cost-per-wear before purchasing
  • Choose quality over quantity
  • Buy classic styles that won't go out of fashion
  • Support brands with transparent supply chains

The most sustainable garment is one that already exists.

  • Explore thrift stores and consignment shops
  • Use apps like Depop, Poshmark, or ThredUp
  • Attend local clothing swaps
  • Check out vintage stores for unique pieces
  • Rent clothes for special occasions

üßµ Care for Your Clothes

Proper care extends the life of your garments.

  • Wash less frequently (air out between wears)
  • Use cold water and eco-friendly detergent
  • Air dry instead of using the dryer
  • Use a Guppyfriend bag to catch microfibers
  • Store clothes properly to prevent damage

üìñ Stay Informed

Knowledge is power.

  • Research brands before buying
  • Check apps like Good On You for brand ratings
  • Follow sustainable fashion advocates
  • Watch documentaries like "The True Cost"
  • Share what you learn with others

üå± Choose Better Materials

When you do buy new, choose wisely.

  • Look for organic cotton (GOTS certified)
  • Choose linen, hemp, or Tencel
  • Opt for recycled materials when possible
  • Avoid synthetic blends that can't be recycled
  • Check for certifications (Fair Trade, OEKO-TEX)

Demand change from brands and policymakers.

  • Ask brands #WhoMadeMyClothes
  • Leave reviews calling for sustainability
  • Support legislation for fashion transparency
  • Vote with your wallet
  • Share your sustainable fashion journey
