Data & Facts

Here's the research-backed data on fashion's environmental impact.

Key Statistics

The numbers behind the fashion industry

Global Emissions

10%

The fashion industry is responsible for 10% of annual global carbon emissions—more than all international flights and maritime shipping combined.

Water Consumption

93B

The industry uses 93 billion cubic meters of water annually—enough to meet the needs of 5 million people.

Textile Waste

92M

92 million tons of textile waste is generated each year—equivalent to a garbage truck full every second.

Microplastics

500K

500,000 tons of microfibers from synthetic clothing enter the ocean annually—equivalent to 50 billion plastic bottles.

Chemical Use

8,000+

Over 8,000 synthetic chemicals are used in fashion manufacturing, many of which are toxic and carcinogenic.

Production Growth

100B

Over 100 billion garments are produced each year—double what was made in 2000. That's 14 items per person on Earth.

Material Matters

Not all fabrics are created equal

🔴 High Impact Materials

  • Conventional Cotton

    Uses 24% of the world's insecticides and 11% of pesticides. Extremely water-intensive.

  • Polyester

    Made from petroleum. Sheds microplastics. Takes 200+ years to decompose.

  • Nylon

    Energy-intensive to produce. Releases nitrous oxide, a greenhouse gas 300x more potent than CO₂.

  • Conventional Leather

    Linked to deforestation. Tanning uses toxic chromium. High water usage.

🟢 Lower Impact Alternatives

  • Organic Cotton

    No pesticides or synthetic fertilizers. Uses up to 91% less water than conventional.

  • Linen

    Made from flax, requires less water and pesticides. Biodegradable.

  • Tencel/Lyocell

    Made from sustainably sourced wood pulp. Closed-loop production process.

  • Recycled Materials

    Recycled polyester uses 59% less energy. Recycled cotton saves water.

  • Hemp

    Requires minimal water, no pesticides, improves soil health. Highly durable.
