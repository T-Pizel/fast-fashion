Material Matters

Not all fabrics are created equal

üî¥ High Impact Materials

  • Conventional Cotton

    Uses 24% of the world's insecticides and 11% of pesticides. Extremely water-intensive.

  • Polyester

    Made from petroleum. Sheds microplastics. Takes 200+ years to decompose.

  • Nylon

    Energy-intensive to produce. Releases nitrous oxide, a greenhouse gas 300x more potent than CO‚ÇÇ.

  • Conventional Leather

    Linked to deforestation. Tanning uses toxic chromium. High water usage.

üü¢ Lower Impact Alternatives

  • Organic Cotton

    No pesticides or synthetic fertilizers. Uses up to 91% less water than conventional.

  • Linen

    Made from flax, requires less water and pesticides. Biodegradable.

  • Tencel/Lyocell

    Made from sustainably sourced wood pulp. Closed-loop production process.

  • Recycled Materials

    Recycled polyester uses 59% less energy. Recycled cotton saves water.

  • Hemp

    Requires minimal water, no pesticides, improves soil health. Highly durable.
